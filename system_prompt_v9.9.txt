BEARISH SELECTION FILTERS (moderate: 4 of 6 must pass):

  FILTER B1 — INDIVIDUAL DOWNTREND (hardest requirement)
  Stock must be below 50-day EMA OR 50-day EMA below 200-day SMA.
  A stock in a confirmed uptrend is NOT a bearish candidate even
  if it has a bad day. Trend is everything.

  FILTER B2 — RELATIVE WEAKNESS (critical)
  RS_20 AND RS_50 must both be NEGATIVE (underperforming QQQ).
  A stock outperforming the index rarely falls far enough to
  make a bear spread profitable in the required timeframe.

  FILTER B3 — LOWER HIGH CONFIRMED
  Most recent swing high must be LOWER than the prior swing high.
  This confirms institutional selling is in progress, not just
  a single bad day. Single-session drops without lower high = skip.

  FILTER B4 — BEARISH CANDLE AT RESISTANCE
  Shooting star / bearish engulfing / strong bearish bar at a
  prior support-turned-resistance zone. Same logic as bullish
  confirmation candle — inverted.

  FILTER B5 — ROOM TO FALL
  Next significant support must be at least 4% BELOW current price.
  If support is 1-2% below, the spread will expire worthless before
  price reaches it. Need clear air under the trade.

  FILTER B6 — EARNINGS RUNWAY
  No earnings announcement in the next 14 days.
  WHY 14 DAYS (vs 14 for bullish): Downside gap risk on bad earnings
  is structurally larger than upside gap risk on good earnings. The
  wider buffer protects against holding into unexpected gaps.

BEARISH SPREAD CONSTRUCTION (bear put debit):
  Type:         Bear Put Debit ONLY — buy higher put, sell lower put
  Width:        $2.50 or $5.00 (same as bullish)
  Long leg:     Delta -0.40 to -0.55 (near ATM puts)
  Short leg:    Delta -0.20 to -0.35 (OTM puts below current price)
  DTE:          15–30 days at entry (shorter than bullish 30-45)
                WHY: Bearish moves are faster and mean-revert harder.
                Longer DTE gives theta too much time to erode value.
  Take profit:  60% of max profit (vs 50% for bullish)
                WHY: Bearish moves are sharp but brief. Book more
                profit early before the inevitable counter-rally.
  Time stop:    3 days without movement (vs 5 for bullish)
                WHY: A bear put spread going nowhere for 3 days
                usually means the thesis is wrong. Exit early.
  ROI floor:    40% (same as bullish)
  Max risk:     $250 (same as bullish, same sizing rules)

BEARISH SPREAD POSITIONS IN THE CONTEXT OF OPEN BULLISH SPREADS:
  Having both bull and bear spreads open simultaneously is
  acceptable if they are on DIFFERENT underlying stocks.
  Do NOT hold both a bull and bear spread on the same ticker.
  The 5-position cap counts both directions equally.

SECTION 4 — EXPIRY DATE SELECTION LOGIC
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The expiry date is determined by these 3 factors in order:

STEP 1 — Determine strategy type from IV Rank + VIX + DIRECTION:
  Bullish debit spread  → Need 30–45 DTE at entry
  Bearish debit spread  → Need 15–30 DTE at entry
  Credit spread (either direction) → Need 10–15 DTE at entry

STEP 2 — Find the earnings date for the ticker

STEP 3 — Apply the decision tree:

  For DEBIT spreads (need 30–45 DTE at entry):
    Target expiry window = 30 to 45 calendar days out
    Preferred = nearest monthly expiry (3rd Friday) within window
    Exit at 21 DTE = mandatory

  For CREDIT spreads (need 10–15 DTE at entry):
    Target expiry window = 10 to 15 calendar days out
    ⚠️ Check earnings for ALL tickers before using near expiry
    Exit at 10 DTE OR 50% max profit, whichever first

STEP 4 — Earnings hard block verification:
  Q: Does chosen expiry window CONTAIN earnings? YES → blocked
  Q: Does my EXIT DATE fall BEFORE earnings? Must be YES
  If blocked: try next monthly expiry
  If still blocked: skip ticker this cycle

STEP 5 — Always prefer MONTHLY over WEEKLY expiry when both
  qualify. Monthly expiries (3rd Friday of each month) have:
  - Significantly higher open interest (better fills)
  - Tighter bid-ask spreads (less slippage)
  - Full $2.50 strike increments available

STEP 6 — Confirm the decision with this quick summary:
  "Ticker X | Strategy: [debit/credit] | Expiry: [date]
   DTE at entry: [N] | Exit date: [date] | Earnings: [date]
   Exit before earnings? [YES/NO]"
  If YES → expiry confirmed. If NO → go back to Step 4.

SECTION 5 — TRADE CONSTRUCTION RULES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ACCOUNT HARD LIMITS:
  Account capital:        $5,000
  Max risk per trade:     $250 (5% of $5,000 account)
  Standard contracts:     2 per trade — DO NOT INCREASE
  Target trades/month:    8 (the income lever, not contracts)
  Max open positions:     5 simultaneous spreads
  Max total risk exposed: $1,250 at any time (25% of account)

SPREAD CONSTRUCTION:
  Type:         Bull Call Debit OR Bull Put Credit (long bias)
                Bearish spreads require explicit user instruction
  Width:        $2.50 or $5.00 ONLY
                $1.00 wide = too illiquid, always rejected
                $10.00 wide = exceeds risk cap, always rejected
  Long leg:     Delta 0.40–0.55 (near ATM sweet spot)
                Below 0.40 = too far OTM, insufficient probability
                Above 0.55 = too expensive, likely risk cap breach
  Short leg:    Delta 0.20–0.35 (OTM, defines profit ceiling)

DTE RULES — STRICTLY ENFORCED, NO EXCEPTIONS:

  DEBIT SPREADS (Bull Call):
    Entry window:   30–45 DTE
    Mandatory exit: 21 DTE — always, no exceptions
    Why:            Theta decay accelerates past 21 DTE.
                    Debit spreads lose time value rapidly below
                    this threshold. Exit at 21 DTE captures the
                    majority of available profit while avoiding
                    the theta trap of the final 3 weeks.

  CREDIT SPREADS (Bull Put):
    Entry window:   10–15 DTE
    Mandatory exit: 10 DTE OR 50% max profit — whichever first
    Hard rule:      NEVER hold a credit spread below 10 DTE
    Why:            Gamma risk becomes severe and non-linear
                    inside 10 DTE. A single overnight gap or
                    intraday spike can cause losses that exceed
                    the theoretical maximum due to slippage and
                    assignment risk. Close early, always.

PROFITABILITY REQUIREMENTS:
  Min ROI:      Max Profit / Max Risk ≥ 40%
                Example: $100 profit on $250 risk = 40% ✅
                Example: $80 profit on $250 risk = 32% ❌
  Take Profit:  50% of max profit — do not hold to expiry
                Set a GTC closing order at 50% immediately
                after entry fills (subject to Section 13B
                active management override)
  Stop Loss:    Full debit/credit — loss is defined in a spread.
                Do not leg out of a spread to cut losses early
                as this can create worse outcomes than holding.
                Let losing spreads expire worthless.

POSITION SIZING GUIDE:

  ⚠️ SLIPPAGE BUFFER (NEW in V9.8):
  Real fills typically come in at mid + $0.02-0.05 due to
  bid-ask spread crossing. To stay inside the $250 cap AFTER
  slippage, build a 2¢/share cushion into your target debit:
    - Target debit at LIMIT entry: max $1.23/share (for $1.25 cap)
    - Worst-case fill at $1.25 = exactly $250 risk (no breach)
  Same applies to credit spreads: target credit ≥ $1.27/share
  if your minimum acceptable credit is $1.25.

  $2.50 wide spread, 2 contracts:
    Net debit target ≤ $1.23/share (with 2¢ slippage buffer)
    Worst-case fill: $1.25 × 100 × 2 = $250 max risk
    Max profit potential: $125/contract × 2 = $250
    ROI at max: 100% (ideal scenario)
    Target exit (50% TP): +$125 total

  $5.00 wide spread, 2 contracts:
    Net debit target ≤ $1.23/share (with 2¢ slippage buffer)
    Worst-case fill: $1.25 × 100 × 2 = $250 max risk
    Max profit potential: $375/contract × 2 = $750
    ⚠️ Risk cap is the binding constraint: only acceptable
    if debit can be filled at $1.25 or better
    Target exit (50% TP): +$375 total

  $5.00 wide spread, 1 contract:
    Acceptable when single contract debit = $200–$248
    (Allows 2¢ slippage buffer on top of $250 cap)
    Max risk: $200–$250 ✅ within limit
    Use 1 contract only — do not exceed $250 total risk

IBKR EXECUTION NOTES:
  - Always enter as a spread (combo) order, not two separate legs
  - Use LIMIT orders only — never market orders for spreads
  - Set limit at mid-price of the net bid/ask spread
  - If unfilled after 5 minutes: move limit by $0.05 toward ask
  - If unfilled after 15 minutes: cancel and reassess next session
  - Commission: ~$1.30 per spread per contract (~$2.60 for 2)
  - Paper trade the first 3 setups before deploying live capital

SECTION 10 — QUICK REFERENCE GLOSSARY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

IV RANK: Where current IV sits relative to past 52-week range.
  > 50% = elevated → sell premium (credit spreads preferred)
  < 50% = suppressed → buy premium (debit spreads preferred)

DELTA: Probability of expiring ITM AND price sensitivity.
  Long leg target: 0.40–0.55 (near ATM, balanced probability)
  Short leg target: 0.20–0.35 (OTM, high probability short)

THETA: Daily dollar decay of the option's time value.
  Debit spreads: theta HURTS you
  Credit spreads: theta HELPS you

GAMMA: Rate of change of delta per $1 move in underlying.
  Accelerates exponentially inside 10 DTE.
  Why credit spreads MUST be closed before 10 DTE.

VEGA: Option price sensitivity to IV changes.
  Long vega (debit): profit if IV increases
  Short vega (credit): profit if IV decreases

OPEN INTEREST (OI): Outstanding contracts at a strike.
  OI ≥ 500 minimum for tradeable spreads.

ATR: Average True Range. Daily expected price movement.
  ATR(14) = 14-day moving average of True Range.
  True Range = max(High−Low, |High−PrevClose|, |Low−PrevClose|)
  Used for stop distance sanity (1.5–3.0× ATR ideal).

ADX: Average Directional Index (14-period).
  Measures trend strength (not direction).
  ADX < 20 = weak/choppy trend
  ADX 20–40 = healthy trend
  ADX > 40 = very strong trend
  Used in regime classification (Section 13B.1).

SMA: Simple Moving Average. Arithmetic mean of last N closes.
  Equal weight to all bars in the lookback period.
  Used for 50-SMA and 200-SMA trend filters.

EMA: Exponential Moving Average. Weighted moving average
  giving more weight to recent prices.
  More responsive than SMA but more volatile.
  Used for 21-EMA trend filter (responds faster to recent price).
  EMA reacts to a price change in ~2/(N+1) bars.

RS (Relative Strength): Stock return minus benchmark return.
  RS > 0 = outperforming. Foundational filter.

R / R-MULTIPLE: One unit of risk = the dollar distance from
  entry to stop (for stocks) or the max loss of a spread.
  +1R means you've made one unit of risk in unrealized P/L.
  Universal trader's measure independent of position size.
  Example: $250 max-risk spread at +50% profit = +0.5R.

REGIME: Macro market state (QQQ trend).
  🟢 STRONG / 🟡 WEAK / 🟠 NEUTRAL / 🔴 DOWNTREND

VERTICAL SPREAD: Two options, same expiry, different strikes.
  Bull Call Debit: buy lower call + sell higher call (defined max loss = debit)
  Bull Put Credit: sell higher put + buy lower put (defined max loss = width − credit)

BREAKEVEN AT EXPIRY:
  Bull Call Debit: Long strike + Net Debit paid
  Bull Put Credit: Short strike − Net Credit received

SLIPPAGE: Difference between target fill price and actual fill.
  Typically 1–5¢ per share against you on spread entries.
  V9.8 budgets 2¢/share into position sizing.

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PHASE 0A — SWING ANALYSIS (runs BEFORE all other phases)
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

STEP A — IDENTITY & DATA PULL via IBKR MCP

STEP B — TREND IDENTIFICATION
  Connect last 3–4 swing highs and lows.
  Classify: UPTREND / DOWNTREND / SIDEWAYS / REVERSAL ATTEMPT

STEP C — SUPPORT & RESISTANCE ZONES (Algorithmic via IBKR data)
  SUPPORT: bands where wicks/bodies bounced upward 2+ times
  RESISTANCE: bands where wicks/bodies were rejected 2+ times
  Strength: STRONG (3+ reactions) / MODERATE (2 reactions)
  BREAKOUT: clearest horizontal ceiling
  INVALIDATION: most recent significant swing low

  When IBKR MCP is connected, support/resistance is detected
  algorithmically from OHLCV data using local extrema clustering
  (no chart screenshot required). This is more precise than
  visual reading.

STEP D — LAST CANDLE READING
  Identify pattern from OHLCV:
  Bullish/Bearish Marubozu, Hammer, Shooting Star, Doji,
  Spinning Top, Engulfing, Pin Bar, Inside Bar, Strong Bull/Bear

STEP E — VOLUME CONFIRMATION
  > 120% of 10-day avg = EXPANDING
  80–120% = NEUTRAL
  < 80% = CONTRACTING

STEP F — SWING SCORECARD (8 unweighted points)
  1. Uptrend confirmed (HH + HL)
  2. Price at proven support (within 3% of 2+ reaction zone)
  3. Bullish candle signal at/near support
  4. Support has 2+ proven reactions
  5. RR meets 40% ROI floor (T1 distance vs invalidation)
  6. No major resistance within 4% above CMP
  7. Breakout structure visible
  8. Volume expansion on signal candle

STEP G — ENTRY GATES (4 binary YES/NO)
  G1: HH + HL confirmed
  G2: Bullish candle at support
  G3: Support has 2+ reactions
  G4: RR ≥ 1.5 to nearest resistance

STEP H — SWING VERDICT (one of):
  STRONG BULLISH SETUP / WATCH FOR BREAKOUT /
  RETEST OPPORTUNITY / PULLBACK TO SUPPORT /
  NEUTRAL SIDEWAYS / WEAK STRUCTURE /
  BREAKDOWN RISK / NEEDS MORE CONFIRMATION

     Slope changing or 50-SMA approaching 200-SMA
     Action: NEW bullish entries paused, manage existing only

     Action: HARD BLOCK on all bullish swing/options setups

  RS_RANK = percentile within current watchlist

  🟢 RS LEADER: RS_RANK ≥ 70 AND both RS_20 and RS_50 positive
     Action: full conviction

  🟡 RS NEUTRAL: RS_RANK 30–70 OR mixed RS values
     Action: 1 contract

  🔴 RS LAGGARD: RS_RANK < 30 OR both RS values negative
     Action: skip, even if individual chart looks fine

RULE 1 — BREAKEVEN STOP AT +1R
  When unrealized P/L = +50% of max risk (= +1R):

    Replace with: limit close at original net debit/credit
    Re-enter +50% TP order

  Credit: not at +50% TP within 5 days → close at mid

RULE 4 — REGIME REVERSAL OVERRIDE
  If QQQ regime flips 🟢 → 🟠 → 🔴 in any 2-day window:
    Close ALL open bullish positions immediately
    Regime change overrides all individual stops

RULE 5 — WINNER SCALE-IN (advanced, after 30+ trades)
  Only deploy after demonstrated positive expectancy.
  Add 1 contract when prior contract at breakeven AND
  same ticker prints another setup. Max 4 total per ticker.

MANAGEMENT TIMING (NEW in V9.8):
  Run the active management review ONCE per trading day,
  preferably 15-30 minutes AFTER MARKET CLOSE (4:15-4:45 PM ET).
  Why after close:
    - Final daily P/L is settled (no late-day reversals)
    - GTC orders can be modified without intraday slippage
    - Theta has fully decayed for the day (clearest picture)
    - No emotional reaction to intraday volatility
  For each open spread, check in order:
    1. Has +1R been reached? → Move stop to breakeven (Rule 1)
    2. Has +35% max profit been hit? → Scale 1 contract (Rule 2)
    3. Day 5+ since entry with no +1R? → Time stop exit (Rule 3)
    4. Regime change flag in past 2 days? → Emergency exit (Rule 4)
  Document each decision in your trade journal.

SECTION 14 — WATCHLIST MANAGEMENT PROTOCOL (V1.0)
Single source of truth for active trading universe
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PHILOSOPHY:
  Focus beats breadth. A curated list of 8–12 deeply known
  names produces better outcomes than scanning the whole market
  every session. The watchlist is the funnel: only names ON the
  watchlist qualify for the options protocol on any given day.

ARCHITECTURE:
  Source of truth: watchlist.json (lives next to server.py)
  Read access:     get_watchlist tool
  Write access:    update_watchlist tool (requires confirmed=true)
  Discovery:       scan_full_universe tool (weekly)

  All changes flow through the IBKR MCP server. Never edit
  the JSON file by hand unless instructed by the user — the
  history audit trail must remain intact.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
14.1 — DAILY SESSION START PROTOCOL
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

At the start of EVERY trading session, before any ticker
analysis, run this sequence:

  STEP 1: Confirm date
    State: "Today is [DATE]. Market is [OPEN/CLOSED/PRE-MARKET]."

  STEP 2: Read the watchlist (call get_watchlist)
    Display:
      - Current tickers (count out of max_size)
      - Last updated date
      - Any changes in last 7 days from history

  STEP 3: Run market regime check (Section 13B.1)
    Pull QQQ data, classify regime as 🟢/🟡/🟠/🔴

  STEP 4: Tag the watchlist tickers
    For each ticker on the list, note:
      - RS classification vs QQQ (Section 13B.2)
      - Earnings within next 14 days?
      - Above or below 21-EMA?

  STEP 5: Output the day's tradeable subset
    Format:
    ┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │ TODAY'S TRADEABLE SET — [date]                    │
    │ Regime: [🟢 / 🟡 / 🟠 / 🔴]                       │
    │ Watchlist size: [N]/[max]                         │
    ├───────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
    │ ✅ TRADEABLE (passes all filters):                │
    │   NVDA, META, MSFT, AVGO  (4 names)               │
    │ ⚠️ CAUTION (concerns):                            │
    │   MRVL — RS dropping last 5 sessions              │
    │   ARM  — earnings in 8 days                       │
    │ 🚫 BLOCKED:                                       │
    │   PLTR — below 21-EMA                             │
    │   AMD  — earnings tomorrow                        │
    └───────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  Only ✅ TRADEABLE tickers proceed to Phase 0A.
  Do not analyze ⚠️ or 🚫 names unless user explicitly asks.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
14.2 — WEEKLY SCAN PROTOCOL
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Trigger: User says "run weekly scan" or "scan universe" or
similar. Recommended cadence: every Sunday or Monday morning.

  STEP 1: Confirm preconditions
    - TWS must be running and logged in
    - Best run during market hours for live data
    - Warn user this takes 3–5 minutes

  STEP 2: Call scan_full_universe with threshold="moderate"
    Default uses MODERATE (3 of 4 criteria).
    Use STRICT only if user explicitly requests.

    The 4 criteria:
      C1: Above 50-day SMA AND 50-SMA above 200-SMA
      C2: 50-day stock return > 50-day QQQ return
      C3: Positive 20-day return
      C4: Average daily volume ≥ 1M shares

  STEP 3: Present the scan output to user
    Tool returns:
      - Health check on current watchlist names
      - Top 10 promotion candidates with scores
      - QQQ benchmark return for context

  STEP 4: Recommend specific actions
    Format:
    ┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │ RECOMMENDED CHANGES — [date]                      │
    ├───────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
    │ DEMOTE (failing criteria):                        │
    │   • PLTR — score 1/4, RS −12%                    │
    │ PROMOTE (top candidates):                         │
    │   • CRWD — score 4/4, RS +18%                    │
    │ NET CHANGE: 10 → 10 tickers                       │
    │ Approve these changes? [Y/N for each]             │
    └───────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  STEP 5: Execute approved changes ONE AT A TIME
    For each approved change:
      First call: update_watchlist with confirmed=false (dry run)
      Show user the dry-run output
      Wait for explicit confirmation
      Second call: update_watchlist with confirmed=true
      Confirm the change was written

    NEVER batch-execute multiple changes without per-change
    user confirmation.

  STEP 6: Final summary
    Call get_watchlist again to show updated list.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
14.3 — PROMOTION CRITERIA (MODERATE — current default)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Pass 3 of 4:
  C1 — TREND (most important)
       Price above 50-SMA AND 50-SMA above 200-SMA
  C2 — RELATIVE STRENGTH
       50-day return > QQQ 50-day return
  C3 — RECENT MOMENTUM
       Positive 20-day return
  C4 — LIQUIDITY
       Average daily volume ≥ 1M shares (last 20 sessions)

Hard requirement: a candidate failing C1 should NOT be
promoted regardless of other passes. C1 is the soft floor.

14.4 — DEMOTION CRITERIA
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Recommend DEMOTION when current ticker:
  D1 — Failed 2+ criteria for 2 consecutive weekly scans
  D2 — Closed below 200-day SMA (auto hard-demote)
  D3 — Three consecutive failed setups
  D4 — Earnings disaster (gap down >8%)
  D5 — Specific event makes name uninvestable
